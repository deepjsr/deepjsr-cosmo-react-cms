"Clean" gets thrown around a lot. Here's where the label helps — and where it misleads.

## Myth 1: Natural is always safer

Poison ivy is natural. The point is *tested and appropriate*, not the origin of an ingredient.

## Myth 2: "Non-toxic" means something regulated

It isn't a legal term in cosmetics. We prefer to list exactly what's in (and out of) every formula.

## Myth 3: Fragrance-free is boring

Fragrance can be a sensitivity trigger. Skipping it is a feature, especially for reactive skin.

## Myth 4: You need a 10-step routine

Skin thrives on consistency, not volume. Three good steps beat ten random ones.

## Myth 5: Expensive = effective

A higher price often pays for packaging and marketing. Look at the active concentration instead.

At Lumière, we'd rather be *clear* than *clean* as a slogan. Read the label, know your skin, and ignore the noise.
